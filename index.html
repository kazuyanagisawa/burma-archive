Voices Under Fire

An archive of testimony, displacement, and survival in Myanmar.

Project Overview

This project gathers letters, testimonies, humanitarian media, and narrative fragments documenting displacement and resilience in Myanmar. The archive explores how human experience survives through fragile records that have been mediated by translation, transcription, OCR, and digital preservation.

Alongside the archive, the project evaluates how AI tools interact with these materials. Methods such as optical character recognition, transcription, translation, and sentiment analysis are tested against real artifacts to examine both their usefulness and their limitations when applied to humanitarian testimony.
